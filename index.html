What are the challenges currently

Lack of availability

The biggest challenge is the lack of available space in urban areas, where growing demand for non-residential spaces has outpaced development.

Limited space

Densely populated urban areas have limited non-residential space, particularly in older parts of cities where small buildings offer little room for new development.

High Cost

The high cost of real estate in many cities is a challenge for individuals and organizations seeking to acquire or rent non- residential space.

Poor infrastructure

Non-residential spaces in India often have poor infrastructure, with limited facilities and space for businesses, leading to inefficient use of available space and difficulty in finding suitable options for operations.

Bureaucratic hurdles

Acquiring space for any usage can be slow and bureaucratic due to regulations and permissions required from government agencies. This discourages individuals and organizations from creating new retail/commercial spaces.

Unorganized sector

A significant portion of non-residential spaces in India are unorganized, with private individuals and small businesses offering such spaces for a fee. This can lead to a lack of regulation and quality control, making it difficult for consumers to find safe and reliable options.

Lack of planning

inadequate planning for non-residential spaces in urban areas is a big challenge as the growing population and urbanization demand more such spaces, but many cities have not kept pace.

Limited access to existing spaces

Limited public access to existing spaces for playing, like parks and playgrounds, may be due to maintenance, safety concerns, or restricted access.

Lack of open spaces

The disappearance of open spaces in cities due to increasing population and urbanization is a significant challenge for finding suitable locations for playing.
